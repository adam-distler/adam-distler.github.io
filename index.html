About Me


I'm Adam Distler, a first-year PhD student in the Harvard Astronomy department. I primarily focus on studying extrasolar planets with Prof. David Charbonneau, but I also enjoy studying dynamics at all scales. I am both a Hertz Fellow and a National Science Foundation Graduate Research Fellow. I did my undergraduate at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, majoring in both Astronomy-Physics and Mathematics, graduating in the Spring of 2024.

I grew up in Linwood Township, and grew to enjoy nature and the outdoors. I spend my free time hiking, fishing, and playing sports such as tennis and golf. Notably, I am the recent winner of the Center for Astrophysics's annual tennis tournament and hope to defend this title in the coming year.

Exoplanet Ages

With Prof. Soares-Furtado, we focus on the detection and characterization of exoplanets with a focus on providing precise age constraints. These planets can serve as benchmarks for theories of formation and evolution. We recently discovered a new exoplanet, TOI-4364b, orbiting a nearby M dwarf that is a Hyades member. We aim to characterize more properties of this system and others, to build a more complete picture of how exoplanet atmospheres, radii, and chemical composition change with time. [1] [2] [3]


Galactic Center Dynamics

A project with Prof. Nick Stone focused on understanding the behavior of compact objects near the centers of galaxies. We aim to characterize their orbital evolution and the occurrence rates of different behavior in preparation for next-generation gravitational wave facilities such as LISA.


Exoplanet Dynamics and Architectures

Work with Prof. Juliette Becker on understanding the origins of exotic types of planets, such as Ultra-Short-Period Planets. With orbital periods of less than one day, we aim to investigate their formation mechanisms and environment.
